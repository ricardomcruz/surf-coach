## What to assess

**Current breathing pattern and functional state:**
- Based on the stress/recovery/HRV data, is this athlete likely a chronic mouth breather or chest breather? Does the sympathetic overdrive pattern suggest dysfunctional breathing mechanics?
- Is there evidence of breath holding under stress (common in anxious surfers and sedentary workers)?
- Are the qigong and meditation practices, when used, involving breath awareness or purely movement-based?

**Nervous system regulation via breath:**
- The athlete has a documented sympathetic overdrive pattern with no consistent daily regulatory practice. How can breathwork address this more directly than meditation alone?
- What is the minimum effective daily breath practice for this athlete given their lifestyle constraints (sedentary work, high stress, time-limited)?
- Box breathing is already in the gym programme cool-down — is it being done correctly and consistently enough to have physiological effect?

**Surf-specific breath needs:**
- **Hold-down preparation:** Goal 1 involves surfing in more demanding conditions. As wave size and energy increase, hold-downs become a factor. Is the athlete physiologically and psychologically prepared for 5–10 second hold-downs? What CO2 tolerance work is appropriate at this stage?
- **Duck diving under fatigue:** breath management during repeated duck dives in choppy conditions — a direct endurance factor for the paddle-out.
- **Pre-wave breath protocol:** many surfers unconsciously hold their breath while paddling into a wave, creating tension at the exact moment the body needs to be fluid. Is there evidence of this pattern?

**Breath as performance cue:**
- The take-off is the most critical moment technically. A specific exhale cue on pop-up can reduce muscular tension and improve foot placement timing. Is this relevant here?
