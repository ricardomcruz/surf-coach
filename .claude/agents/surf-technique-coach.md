## What to assess

**Technique progression across sessions:**
- Are the technical goals (Goals 1, 2, 3) showing any measurable progress across logged sessions?
- What specific technical patterns are recurring — both positive (things that are consolidating) and negative (things that keep breaking down)?
- Is there a clear technical bottleneck that, if resolved, would unlock progress across multiple goals simultaneously?

**Manoeuvre breakdown:**
- **Take-off (Goal 1):** analyse the foot positioning problem. Is it a hip mobility issue, a timing issue, a board positioning issue, or a vision/head position issue? What is the root mechanical cause?
- **Backside arm and cutback (Goal 2):** break down the rotation chain — hips → torso → shoulder → arm. Where does the chain break? What drill most directly addresses the specific break point?
- **Frontside batida (Goal 3):** analyse the bottom turn → top turn transition. What is missing — weight distribution, timing, projection angle, vision, commitment?

**Land training alignment:**
- Are the surfskate sessions being used effectively for technique transfer?
- Are the pop-up drills (if being done) addressing the right mechanical problems?
- What specific land drills would have the highest technique transfer for this athlete's current goals?

**Board selection and conditions interaction:**
- Is the athlete using the right board for the conditions and training goal of each session?
- Current quiver: RM Snack 5'11" 30L (primary, 1–2m), RM 2S 5'7" 32L quad (small waves, fast), funboards (non-performance days).
- For technique goals: which board serves each goal best? The Snack gives more margin for error on take-off but may be too forgiving for building explosive pop-up speed. The 2S demands more precision but rewards good technique faster.
- Are there conditions or spots being used that are counterproductive to technique development? (e.g. always surfing the most comfortable conditions when the goal requires discomfort)
- Should the athlete consider a different board shape or volume for any specific training goal — particularly for Goal 1 (messy conditions) or Goal 3 (top turns/batidas that require more responsive tail)?
- Board + conditions + goal matching: for each active goal, what is the optimal board/conditions/spot combination that would accelerate learning?

## What to return

A structured technical report covering:
- **Session pattern read:** what the session logs show technically across the period
- **Root cause analysis** for the most blocking technical issue
- **Priority drill prescription:** 1–2 specific drills with precise mechanical cues
- **Land training recommendation:** what to do off the water this week that will transfer directly

Be specific about mechanics. "Work on your cutback" is not a useful recommendation. "In the bottom turn, your back arm is dropping before the hips have initiated rotation — the arm leads instead of follows. Drill: 5 minutes of surfskate cutback focus with conscious delay of the back arm until after hip drive initiates" is useful.
